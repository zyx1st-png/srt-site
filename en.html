An Independent Research Program

Selective Reality Theory

选择性现实理论 · SRT

Reality is not first given and then chosen; it becomes determinate through choice under constraint. SRT asks how possibilities become actual — and why the actual hardens and thickens until it looks as if it had always been there.

Site language: ChinesePapers: English2026
01 / WHAT IT IS NOT

Three standing disclaimers

The fastest way to locate SRT is by what it refuses.

Not idealism

Not “whatever I think becomes real.” Every choice must pass through constraint, resistance, cost, and the test of consequences. A private imagining that cannot stabilize, bear load, or leave traceable consequences does not count as reality.

Not relativism

Not “everyone's reality is equally true.” Reality has thickness: the more a structure stays stable across subjects, positions, time, and perturbation, the more real it is. SRT studies exactly these thicknesses, cracks, and costs.

Not panpsychism

Not “everything is conscious.” Choice is everywhere, but consciousness has a threshold: concern, consequences that return, self-modulation, persistence. Filtering information and producing outputs does not automatically amount to consciousness.
